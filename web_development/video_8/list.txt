Block-level Elements (used most first)

<div> – Generic container, for grouping content and layout.

<p> – Paragraph text. Used for most content blocks.

<h1> … <h6> – Headings. Organize content hierarchy.

<form> – Collect user input (login, signup, feedback).

<ul> / <ol> / <li> – Lists (unordered and ordered).

<section> – Logical sections of a page (like main parts).

<header> – Top of page or section (navigation, logo).

<footer> – Bottom of page (contact info, copyright).

<article> – Independent content, like a blog post.

<aside> – Sidebar or related content.

<table> / <tr> / <td> / <th> / <thead> / <tbody> / <tfoot> – Tabular data.

<blockquote> – Large quotation block.

<pre> – Preformatted text (keeps spaces/line breaks).

-----------------------------------------------------------

 Inline Elements (used most first)

<span> – Generic inline wrapper for styling or JS.

<a> – Hyperlinks. Essential for navigation.

<strong> – Important text (bold emphasis).

<em> – Emphasized text (italic, for meaning).

<b> / <i> / <u> – Visual text formatting.

<img> – Images inside content.

<input> / <label> / <textarea> / <select> / <button> – Form elements.

<code> – Inline code snippet.

<abbr> – Abbreviations.

<cite> – Citation text.

<q> – Short inline quotations.

<time> – Date/time in machine-readable format.

<mark> – Highlighted text
